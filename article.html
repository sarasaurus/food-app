Parenting is hard

You don't have to plan a party... just have it at McDonald's.

Because cleaning up sucks, no one has time to make goodie bags, and you have a million other things to do.

By Angie McDowell
March 14, 2022

If you were born and raised in the 90s, McDonald's birthday parties were the best present you could get. It might be hard to believe now, but there really was a time when we actively looked forward to rolling around in a germ-filled ball pit and eating French fries that had been touched by as many hands as physically possible. How times change. However, no matter what the current social stigma attached to partying might be, McDonald's birthday parties were and always will be awesome. So, if you can't stomach the thought of themed arts and craft activities, or decorating your living room with dollar store decorations — then just don't do it. Have the party at McDonald's.

Easy party idea #2: Everyone loves pizza.

Ok, we get it. Maybe McDonald's maybe isn't the best place to throw a hands-off brithday party, especially if your family is vegetarian, enjoys sanitary playspaces, or whatever else it may be. Easy party option number two: old school pizza party. Order a bunch of pizza, take it to a public park, and let the kids run wild for a few hours. If you're feeling up to it (or the weather is looking like shit) most parks have a picnic shelter you can rent. Bonus: cleanup is minimal and local wildlife will love all the pizza crust that unavoidably gets dropped on the ground.

Don't make the cake from scratch.

Repeat after us: do not make a birthday cake from scratch. You could labor for hours (maybe even days) creating a beautiful cake onyl for it to be destroyed in seconds. Go to your local grocery store, get a dozen cupcakes or a sheet cake from the bakery, stick some candles on top and call it good. There are plenty of ways you can choose to spend your precious free time, and we bet that creating an overly elaborate and time-consuming cake isn't at the top of the list. And let's not kid ourselves here. Kid's are assholes and prefer the Paw Patrol cake from Safeway to your Pinterest fail version. If homemade baked goods are a must at this upcoming party, please, we implore you, just use a boxed cake mix and slap some Betty Crocker frosting on there. It's going to taste fine. And everyone will eat it because they will eat what you give them, or eat nothing at all.

Bring the beverages... for kids and adults.

You know we aren't referring to Minute Maid. Throwing any party for any number of kids is an effort, and whether it goes off smoothly without a hitch or the meltdown of the year happens before the candles are blown out, we think you deserve a drink. Celebrate after the party (or during — we don't judge) with a grown up beverage and your favorite parent-friend turned best-friend. You earned it.
